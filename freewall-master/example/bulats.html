Preparing for BULATS

Ian Cawley, Cambridge ESOL's Marketing Manager: Sales Support and Training, give a presentation on the BULATS exam. He has useful tips for candidates and teachers alike.

 

What is BULATS?

BULATS is a multilingual set of workplace language assessment, training and benchmarking tools. It is used internationally for business and industry recruitment, for identifying and delivering training, for admission to study business-related courses and for assessing the effectiveness of language courses and training.

Part 1: Interview

The interview tests ability to use language for social purposes, e.g. making introductions, answering questions, stating an opinion.

Part 1 of the BULATS Speaking test takes 4 minutes. The examiner will ask you warm-up questions perhaps to find out where you come from, your interests or your travel experiences, and depending upon your status at the time, questions about your job or your studies.

Part 2: Presentation

Tests ability to: sustain a long turn, speaking coherently, use language to state an opinion, describe, compare and contrast etc.

Part 2 of the BULATS Speaking test lasts about 4 minutes. The examiner gives you a sheet with 3 topics on work-related subjects. You'll be asked to speak about one of the topics for 1 minute without interruption. You'll have 1 minute to prepare. At the end of your talk the examiner will ask you one or two questions related to the topic.

Part 3: Information Exchange and Discussion

Tests ability to: ask for information, initiate discussion, ask for and give opinions, agree and disagree, develop comments made by others and generate new ideas.

Part 3 of the exam, which lasts about 4 minutes, it consists of two sections. The examiner will hand you a paper describing the context for a role play and a discussion. The examiner will participate in the role play and you will have to ask him/her questions in order to get the information the role play requires. This will lead on to a discussion between you and the examiner on a work-related topic.
