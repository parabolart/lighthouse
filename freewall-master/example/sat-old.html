What is LAP?

The Language Assessment Profile interview is usually called the LAP Test. It is a procedure that measures a person’s functional speaking ability by determining an individual’s strengths and weaknesses. The interview tests a trainee’s ability to use the English language effectively in real life situations.

The LAP Test is a 20 minute recorded test. It takes place in a private room with a certified examiner. The LAP Test has 4 parts; the warm-up, the level check, the probes and the cool-down. The scores range from 1-6, 1 being a novice-low score, and 6 being a native speaker’s level. The requirement at LG is 3.5, a intermediate-high score, and 4.0 is the most desired score and one of the most difficult scores to reach.
